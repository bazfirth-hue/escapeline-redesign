For Parents & Families

Something doesn't feel right.
Trust that feeling.

You don't need proof. You don't need all the answers. If something about your child's behaviour has changed and your gut is telling you something is wrong — that is enough to call us. Our helpline is free, confidential, and run by people who understand exactly what you are going through.

  • Unexplained money, gifts, or new possessions
  • Sudden changes in mood, behaviour, or friends
  • New, older friends you don't recognise
  • Staying out very late or going missing overnight
  • Becoming secretive about their phone
  • Withdrawing from family and longstanding friends
  • Out-of-character anger, fear, or anxiety

For Schools & Professionals

Your students are being targeted.
Here's how to stop it.

We deliver hard-hitting, age-appropriate workshops and professional training developed by people who have worked on the front line of exploitation — and by experts who've lived it. Our sessions give children, and the adults around them, the knowledge and strategies to recognise and resist grooming before it takes hold.

  • Primary school workshops (Years 4, 5 & 6)
  • Secondary school assemblies & workshops (Years 7–13)
  • Professional training: social workers, youth workers, health staff, police
  • 7-week targeted 1-to-1 support programme
  • Parent & carer awareness evenings

Common Questions

You're not the first parent to feel this way.

Yes. Children being exploited will almost always deny it — to parents, teachers, and police. This isn't deception; it's the result of grooming, fear, and in many cases, a genuine loyalty to their exploiters that has been carefully built over time.

Their denial does not mean you are wrong. It often means the grooming is working exactly as the gang intended. Trust your instincts, not their reassurances.

It's a genuinely hard question, and one our helpline can help you think through. The key difference is usually pattern and combination: one warning sign in isolation might be normal teenage behaviour; several warning signs appearing together, or an unexplained sudden change, is worth a conversation.

Particularly significant: unexplained money or gifts, new older friends you can't identify, and a child who seems frightened of something they won't name. You know your child. Call us.

Our helpline is independent, confidential, and run by Escapeline — not by the police. We will not share information about your call with any authority without your knowledge and agreement, except in circumstances where there is an immediate risk to life.

Your child is a victim, not a criminal. Our role is to support your family, help you understand your options, and advocate for the right safeguarding response.

County lines is the name given to organised criminal networks that use a dedicated phone line — the "line" — to sell drugs from a city into smaller towns and rural areas. The gangs recruit young people to transport and sell drugs along these routes.

They target young people specifically because they are less likely to be stopped, searched, or prosecuted. Grooming begins with gifts, attention, and a sense of belonging — often long before the young person realises what is happening. By the time they do, debt bondage, threats, and psychological control make it very hard to leave.

It is never too late. We work with young people across a wide age range and with their families. Older teenagers are often at greater risk because statutory protections reduce as they approach 18 — which is exactly when gangs may escalate pressure on them.

Please call us whatever your child's age.
